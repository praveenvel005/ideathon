Rules & Guidelines

📌 Ideathon Rules & Restrictions

  • Teams must have 4 members (only 3rd & 4th year IT students).
  • Each student can join only in one team.
  • Ideas must relate to the given domains/problem statements.
  • No plagiarism – solutions must be original and developed during the event.
  • Use of open-source tools and free APIs is encouraged.
  • Teams must submit a 1-page abstract before development starts.
  • Final submission must include:
    • Working demo/prototype
    • Presentation slides
    • Source code/documentation
  • Total pitching time per will be 10 mins(including Q&A).
  • Code/solution sharing between teams is prohibited.
  • Violation of rules leads to disqualification.

⚖ Do’s & Don’ts

✅ Do’s

  • Form teams of 4 members from 3rd & 4th year IT.
  • Stick to the given domains/problem statements.
  • Use open-source tools, free APIs, and frameworks.
  • Submit a 1-page abstract before coding.
  • Practice time management to finish within the event schedule.
  • Acknowledge any third-party libraries or resources used.

❌ Don’ts

  • Don’t plagiarize or copy existing projects directly.
  • Don’t work on offensive, unethical, or illegal ideas.
  • Don’t share code/solutions between teams.
  • Don’t exceed the presentation time limit (5–10 minutes).
  • Don’t submit incomplete work without a demo/prototype.
  • Don’t rely solely on AI tools — the core idea must be yours.
  • Don’t miss deadlines for abstract or final submission.

⚠ Non-compliance with these rules may lead to disqualification.
